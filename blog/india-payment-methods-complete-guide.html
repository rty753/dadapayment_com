India Payment Methods — The Complete Guide for Merchants

March 10, 2025 • By Dada Payment Team

Overview of India's Payment Landscape

India has undergone a dramatic transformation in digital payments over the past decade. From a predominantly cash-based economy, the country has emerged as one of the world's largest digital payment markets, with over 100 billion digital transactions processed annually. Government initiatives like Digital India and demonetization in 2016 acted as catalysts, pushing hundreds of millions of consumers toward electronic payment methods.

For merchants looking to enter or expand in the Indian market, understanding the local payment ecosystem is critical. Unlike Western markets where credit and debit cards dominate, India's payment landscape is fragmented across multiple platforms and technologies — each with its own user base, fee structure, and technical integration requirements.

This guide covers every major payment method available in India, compares their key attributes, and provides practical advice on which methods to prioritize for your business.

UPI (Unified Payments Interface)

UPI is the backbone of digital payments in India. Developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI), UPI enables instant bank-to-bank transfers through mobile devices using a Virtual Payment Address (VPA). As of early 2025, UPI processes over 10 billion transactions per month, accounting for more than 75% of all retail digital payments in India.

Key Features

  • Instant settlement: Funds transfer in real time, 24/7, including weekends and holidays.
  • No transaction fees: The Indian government has mandated zero merchant discount rates (MDR) on UPI transactions, making it effectively free for merchants.
  • Interoperability: Customers using any UPI-enabled app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, or bank apps) can pay any UPI-accepting merchant.
  • Transaction limit: Up to INR 1,00,000 per transaction for most categories; higher limits available for specific use cases like education and healthcare.

When to Use UPI

UPI should be your primary payment method for the Indian market. It works for all transaction types — e-commerce, subscriptions, in-app purchases, bill payments, and peer-to-peer transfers. Given its dominance and zero fees, there is rarely a reason not to offer UPI.

Paytm

Paytm is one of India's largest digital payment platforms with over 350 million registered users. While Paytm transactions increasingly route through UPI on the backend, the Paytm ecosystem includes its own wallet, Paytm Postpaid (buy-now-pay-later), and an extensive merchant network.

Key Features

  • Paytm Wallet: A closed-loop prepaid wallet that customers can load via bank transfer, cards, or UPI. Useful for small-value transactions.
  • Paytm Postpaid: A credit line product that allows customers to buy now and pay later, increasing conversion rates for merchants.
  • QR code payments: Paytm's QR ecosystem is widely used by small and large merchants alike across India.
  • Integration: Paytm offers SDKs and APIs for web and mobile integration, plus a hosted checkout page for quick deployment.

When to Use Paytm

Paytm is valuable when you want to capture customers who prefer wallet payments or buy-now-pay-later options. It is particularly effective in e-commerce, food delivery, travel booking, and entertainment verticals where Paytm Postpaid can boost order values.

PhonePe

PhonePe is India's leading UPI app by transaction volume, commanding roughly 48% of UPI market share. Owned by Walmart (via Flipkart), PhonePe has over 500 million registered users and processes billions of transactions monthly.

Key Features

  • UPI-first: PhonePe is built entirely on UPI, meaning transactions settle instantly via the banking network.
  • PhonePe Switch: A mini-app platform that allows merchants to host lightweight versions of their apps inside PhonePe, reaching its massive user base without a separate download.
  • Insurance and investments: PhonePe has expanded into financial services, giving it sticky daily engagement with users.
  • Merchant solutions: PhonePe offers POS devices, payment links, and SDKs for online and offline merchant integration.

When to Use PhonePe

Since PhonePe operates on UPI rails, accepting UPI automatically means you accept PhonePe payments. However, direct integration with PhonePe's merchant tools can provide additional benefits like in-app placement and promotional support.

IMPS and NEFT

IMPS (Immediate Payment Service) and NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer) are bank-to-bank transfer systems managed by the RBI and NPCI.

IMPS

  • Speed: Instant transfers, available 24/7.
  • Limit: Up to INR 5,00,000 per transaction.
  • Fees: Banks charge INR 2.50 to INR 25 depending on the transaction amount.
  • Use case: Best for high-value B2B payments and payouts where UPI limits are insufficient.

NEFT

  • Speed: Settled in batches every 30 minutes, 24/7 since December 2019.
  • Limit: No upper limit on transaction amount.
  • Fees: Minimal — typically INR 2.50 to INR 25 depending on amount and bank.
  • Use case: Ideal for large B2B transfers, vendor payments, and salary disbursements where instant settlement is not critical.

When to Use IMPS and NEFT

These methods are less common for consumer-facing checkout flows but essential for payout operations, vendor settlements, and high-value transactions that exceed UPI limits. Most payment gateways, including Dada Payment's system, support IMPS and NEFT for merchant payouts.

NetBanking

NetBanking allows customers to pay directly from their bank's online banking portal. While its share has declined significantly since UPI's rise, NetBanking remains relevant for certain user segments — particularly older consumers and corporate buyers who prefer browser-based banking.

Key Features

  • Wide bank coverage: Most payment gateways support NetBanking from 50+ Indian banks.
  • Higher transaction limits: NetBanking limits are set by the individual bank and can be significantly higher than UPI limits, making it suitable for big-ticket purchases.
  • No app required: Customers complete the payment entirely within their browser, without needing a smartphone or UPI app.
  • Fees: Merchant fees typically range from 1% to 2% of the transaction amount, plus a flat fee per transaction.

When to Use NetBanking

Offer NetBanking as a fallback option for customers who do not use UPI or mobile wallets. It is especially important for B2B platforms, education fee payments, and insurance premium collection where transaction values can be high.

How to Choose the Right Payment Methods

Selecting payment methods for your Indian operations depends on several factors:

  1. Know your customer: If your audience is predominantly mobile-first consumers, prioritize UPI. For B2B or enterprise clients, add NetBanking and NEFT/IMPS.
  2. Consider transaction values: UPI works well for transactions up to INR 1 lakh. For higher amounts, ensure NetBanking and IMPS are available.
  3. Optimize for conversion: Offering multiple payment methods reduces checkout abandonment. Customers are more likely to complete a purchase when they see their preferred method.
  4. Evaluate costs: UPI's zero-MDR policy makes it the most cost-effective option. Wallet and BNPL options carry fees but can boost conversion and average order value.
  5. Plan for payouts: If you need to send money to users (refunds, withdrawals, commissions), ensure your gateway supports IMPS and NEFT payout APIs.

Pakistan Digital Payments Overview

Pakistan is one of Asia's most exciting frontier markets for digital payments. With a population exceeding 220 million people and a median age under 23, the country presents an enormous opportunity for merchants and platforms looking to tap into a young, mobile-first consumer base. Despite relatively low traditional banking penetration — with only around 30 percent of adults holding a formal bank account — Pakistan's mobile money ecosystem has surged ahead, driven by affordable smartphones, expanding 4G networks, and progressive regulatory support from the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP).

The mobile wallet landscape is dominated by two major players: JazzCash and Easypaisa. Together, these two platforms serve well over 100 million registered accounts, making them the primary on-ramp for digital commerce across the country. JazzCash, operated by Jazz (formerly Mobilink), is the largest mobile financial services provider in Pakistan, offering consumers a straightforward way to send money, pay bills, and make purchases online. Easypaisa, backed by Telenor Microfinance Bank, was the pioneer of mobile money in Pakistan and continues to hold a substantial share of the market, particularly in semi-urban and rural regions where traditional banking infrastructure is sparse.

Beyond mobile wallets, Pakistan's banking sector is undergoing a digital transformation. The SBP has actively promoted electronic payments through several landmark initiatives. The most significant of these is Raast, Pakistan's first instant payment system, launched in 2021. Raast enables real-time, person-to-person and person-to-merchant transfers between bank accounts and wallets using a simple ID such as a mobile number or CNIC (national identity number). Raast is modeled after India's UPI and is expected to be a transformative force for Pakistan's digital economy, reducing reliance on cash and bringing millions more consumers into the formal financial system.

Other SBP initiatives, including the National Payment Systems Strategy and the push for QR-code-based merchant payments, are further accelerating the shift toward cashless transactions. The SBP has also introduced the Micropayment Gateway, designed to facilitate low-value digital transactions at scale, and has actively encouraged banks and fintechs to develop interoperable payment solutions. For any merchant or platform looking to accept payments in Pakistan, understanding this rapidly evolving ecosystem is essential — and having a payment gateway system that supports JazzCash, Easypaisa, bank transfers, and Raast from day one is a significant competitive advantage.

Pakistan Compliance & Regulatory Framework

Navigating SBP regulations, PSO/PSP licensing, and AML/KYC requirements with confidence.

Operating a Pakistan payment gateway requires strict adherence to the regulatory framework established by the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP). The SBP oversees all payment system operators (PSOs) and payment service providers (PSPs) under the Payment Systems and Electronic Fund Transfers Act, 2007, and the subsequent regulations issued under this act. Any entity facilitating electronic payments in Pakistan must either hold a PSO or PSP license directly or operate through a licensed partner.

Dada Payment operates in full compliance with SBP regulations, ensuring that all transactions processed through our Pakistan payment gateway meet the required legal and technical standards. Our compliance framework covers several critical areas:

PSO/PSP Licensing

We work through licensed PSO and PSP partners to ensure every transaction is processed within the legal framework mandated by the SBP. Merchants integrating through Dada Payment do not need to obtain their own PSO/PSP license, as our infrastructure provides the necessary regulatory coverage.

AML & KYC

All merchants undergo rigorous Know Your Customer (KYC) verification before activation. We implement anti-money laundering (AML) screening procedures in line with SBP guidelines and the Financial Monitoring Unit (FMU) requirements. Transaction monitoring systems flag suspicious activity in real time for review by our compliance team.

Data Security

Our systems are PCI DSS compliant, ensuring that cardholder data and sensitive payment information are handled according to international security standards. All data transmitted through our Pakistan payment gateway is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher, and we conduct regular security audits and penetration testing.

Reporting & Record-Keeping

We maintain comprehensive transaction records as required by SBP and make them available for audit purposes. Merchants receive detailed settlement reports and can access real-time transaction logs through their dashboard, ensuring full transparency and traceability for every PKR transaction.

For merchants in industries that face additional regulatory scrutiny — such as gaming, digital goods, or cross-border services — our high-risk payment gateway solution includes enhanced compliance features tailored to meet the specific requirements of these verticals within the Pakistani regulatory context.
